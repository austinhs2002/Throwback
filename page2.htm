AOL (America Online) was one of the most popular internet service providers in the 1990s and early 2000s. Beyond providing dial-up internet, it offered email, instant messaging, and curated content for users exploring the early web.


AOL helped millions of people access the internet for the first time and popularized features like chat rooms and email. Its AOL Instant Messenger (AIM) was particularly influential in shaping online communication and social networking.
Fun fact: AIM, AOL's instant messaging service, inspired many modern messaging apps and introduced early online culture like away messages and buddy lists.
