ICQ was one of the first instant messaging programs of the Internet era, launched in 1996. It allowed users to send real-time messages, track when friends were online, and organize contacts through unique user numbers.

ICQ introduced features like chat rooms, away messages, and notifications for friend logins. It became extremely popular worldwide and influenced nearly all modern chat apps that followed.

Click here for a fun ICQ fact! The name “ICQ” sounds like “I seek,” reflecting its original goal of helping users find and connect with friends online.
