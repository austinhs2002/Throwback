Netscape Navigator was one of the first widely used web browsers in the 1990s. It popularized graphical browsing on the Internet and helped millions experience the web beyond text-based interfaces.

Netscape introduced many features we take for granted today, including cookies, bookmarks, and secure HTTPS connections. It became the browser of choice for early internet users before the rise of Internet Explorer.

Click here for a fun Netscape fact! Netscape's mascot, the green dinosaur named Mozilla, inspired the name of the modern Mozilla Foundation and Firefox browser.
